Simone de Beauvoir

Her Life.

1908:Beauvoir was born on 9 January 1908 in the 6th arrondissement of Paris.

1910:Simone's sister, Hélène was born on 6 June 1910.

1925:Beauvoir finished highschool at 17 passing baccalaureate exams in mathematics and philosophy.

1929:Beauvoir becomes the youngest person to pass the agrégation in philosophy at the École Normale Supérieure, only narrowly coming in second place to Jean-Paul Sartre. This would mark the beginning of her romantic relationship with Sartre which would last the next 51 years until his death in 1980.

1929-1943:Beauvoir taught mainly in high schools during this period until she could support herself fully on the earnings of her writings.

1950s-1960s:Beauvoir published many essays and pieces of fiction during this time. During this time, Beauvoir wrote a four-volume autobiography which included Memoires of a Dutiful Daughter,The Prime of Life, Force of Circumstances and All Said and Done

1970s:Beauvoir becomes active in the French women's liberation movement. In 1971, she authored and signed the Manifesto fo the 343 - a list of well-known women who claimed to have had an abortion which was then illegal in France.

1986:Beauvoir died of pneumonia on 14 April 1986 in Paris at the age of 78.

Her Legacy.

Beauvoir had an instrumental role in modern feminist philosophy especially with her book The Second Sex which was foundational in the gender-sex distinction in popular discourse. She argued "One is not born, but rather becomes, a woman" which helped launch second-wave feminism by establishing gener as a social construct rather than an inherent biological characteristic. She was also an active figure in other areas such as existentialism, literature, and social theory.
